Project and team

The Baltic Health Index is a regional study applying the Ocean Health Index framework. The aim is to initiate the development of an integrative and transparent tool that can be used by decision-makers to guide management of the Baltic Sea region towards increased sustainability.

The Baltic Health Index is a project jointly led by Stockholm Resilience Centre (SRC) and Baltic Eye at the Baltic Sea Centre, both at Stockholm University, together with the Ocean Health Index team. The project is funded by Baltic Ecosystem Adaptive Management (BEAM, a research programme on ecosystem-based management of the Baltic Sea), Baltic Eye and the Johansson Family Foundation. The Baltic Health Index formally started in 2015 and will run until mid 2017. A trans-disciplinary project, it involves many international researchers from around the Baltic Sea.

The project is led by Thorsten Blenckner at SRC. Researchers appointed as Goalkeepers are responsible for one goal each, and work with teams of researchers who are experts on subjects central to each goal.
